The Food Security Problem (Kedah, Malaysia)

Why Do We Need Derivatives in Paddy Cultivation?

To guarantee rice self-sufficiency in Malaysia (aiming for over 75% SSL), agro-technologists must precisely schedule resource distribution. Paddy growth is not linear; it follows a sigmoidal (logistic) pattern. By monitoring the plant's rate of growth—represented mathematically as the first derivative \(f'(t)\) of height or biomass over time—we can pinpoint exact biological transition phases. This tells farmers precisely when to apply Nitrogen fertilizers (Urea) and when to manage water levels in the paddy fields (bendang).

Empirical Base: • MARDI Rice Cultivation Manual (MR297) • DOA Malaysia Paddy Production Statistics
Mathematical Modeling

The Growth Functions

Height Function \(f(t)\):

$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$

Growth Rate (Derivative) \(f'(t)\):

$$f'(t) = k \cdot f(t) \left(1 - \frac{f(t)}{L}\right)$$
Where \(L\) = Max Height, \(k\) = growth rate constant, and \(t_0\) = inflection point (day of peak growth).
